The Story Behind the Smoke

Meet Chef Ken Hatfield.
Southern Born. Grandmother-Taught.

Chef Ken Hatfield didn't learn Southern cooking from a culinary school or a cookbook. He learned it the way it was meant to be learned — from his grandmother, standing in her kitchen in the South, absorbing techniques and flavors that stretch back over 150 years through his family. Those aren't just recipes. That's a lineage.

With over 30 years of professional cooking experience, Ken has cooked for dignitaries and guests from around the world — including aboard the Africa Mercy — and earned national recognition with an appearance on Food Network's Chopped and a recurring segment on Fox8's New Day Cleveland. He has cooked at the highest levels. And yet every dish at Hatfield's still tastes like it came from that same grandmother's kitchen — because it did.

The recipes on our menu aren't new — they're over 150 years old, passed down through generations of Southern cooks who never once wrote them down, because they didn't need to. They just knew. Every bite carries that weight and that history.

"I put every bit of me into my cooking. People say they can taste my dedication when they eat one of my dishes." — Chef Ken Hatfield

The Family Behind the Flavor

Meet the Hatfield's Crew

This isn't just a restaurant — it's a family operation built on Southern values, mutual respect, and a shared love for food done right. Every person on this team carries the Hatfield's standard with pride.

Ken Hatfield

Executive Chef / Owner

Born and raised in the South, Ken learned to cook at his grandmother's side — absorbing techniques and flavors that stretch back 150 years through his family. After 35+ years as a professional chef — including stints at the House of Blues Chicago and aboard the Africa Mercy hospital ship — he brought those roots home to Cleveland. Featured on Food Network's Chopped and Fox8's New Day Cleveland, Ken's cooking is both nationally recognized and deeply personal. Every plate he sends out carries his family's legacy.

Jessica Hatfield

Director of Operations / Owner

Jessi is the engine that keeps Hatfield's running at full throttle. As co-owner and Director of Operations, she oversees everything behind the scenes — staffing, systems, guest experience, and the relentless pursuit of excellence that guests feel the moment they walk in. She believes that great hospitality is just as important as great food, and she makes sure Hatfield's delivers both, every single time.

Maw

CFO / Owner

Every great family business has a matriarch — and Maw is ours. As CFO and co-owner, she is the financial backbone and spiritual foundation of the Hatfield family enterprise. Her Southern wisdom, sharp instincts, and unshakeable sense of values are woven into every decision we make. When she says it's good, it's good. We don't question Maw. We just say thank you.

Scarlet

Front of House

Scarlet is the warm Southern welcome you feel the moment you walk through our door. With a natural gift for hospitality and a genuine love for every guest, she has a way of making strangers feel like regulars and regulars feel like family. Her energy sets the tone for the entire dining room — and it's always a good one.

Emily Rose

General Manager

Emily Rose runs the house — and she runs it right. As General Manager, she's the one making sure every plate hits the table on time, every guest leaves with a smile, and every team member brings their best every shift. She carries the Hatfield's standard with quiet authority and genuine care. A fierce protector of the guest experience, Emily Rose is the reason things run as smoothly as they do.

Little Jess

Front of House

Don't let the name fool you — Little Jess is a big part of what makes Hatfield's feel like home. Her warmth, quick wit, and genuine attention to every guest turns first-time visitors into loyal regulars. She's proof that great front-of-house isn't about scripts or procedures — it's about people, and she's a natural.

Love what you see? We're always looking for good people.
